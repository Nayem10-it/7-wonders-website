The 7 Wonders of the World

Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest pyramid in Egypt.

  • Built around 2560 BC.
  • It was the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years.
  • Constructed from over 2 million stone blocks.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is an ancient arena in Rome, Italy.

  • Construction began in 72 AD and completed in 80 AD.
  • Could hold up to 50,000 spectators.
  • Used for gladiator fights and public events.

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China stretches over 21,000 km.

  • Built between the 7th century BC and 16th century.
  • Constructed to protect China from invasions.
  • Made of stone, brick, and tamped earth.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city in Peru.

  • Built in the 15th century.
  • Located 2,430 meters above sea level.
  • Rediscovered by Hiram Bingham in 1911.

Petra

Petra is a famous archaeological city in Jordan.

  • Known as the “Rose City” because of the color of its rock.
  • Established around 312 BC by the Nabataeans.
  • Famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system.

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza is a large Mayan archaeological site in Mexico.

  • Built between 600–1200 AD.
  • Famous for the Kukulkan Pyramid (El Castillo).
  • Important center of Mayan political and economic life.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum in India.

  • Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of Mumtaz Mahal.
  • Construction started in 1632 and completed in 1653.
  • Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.
